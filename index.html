About Me

A little bit
about me

I grew up fascinated by two things: how the body works and how computers think. That curiosity led me to Johns Hopkins, where I completed dual degrees in Computer Science and Biomedical Engineering. Now at Stanford, I'm putting those threads together in the context of healthcare AI.

My work lives in the messy, exciting space where machine learning meets clinical practice. I care deeply about building models that are not just accurate but equitable — systems that work just as well for every patient, regardless of who they are.

Outside of research, I find myself thinking a lot about the epistemics of medicine, reading history of science, and making very strong opinions about coffee.

Research

What I'm working on

01
Fries Lab · Clinical NLP

Retrieval-Augmented Clinical Reasoning for Prognosis Modeling

Investigating whether querying over a patient population helps LLMs reason more effectively about individual patient EHR timelines. Using BM25 retrieval over EHRSHOT benchmarks to surface clinically relevant population context at inference time — without any fine-tuning.

RAG Clinical LLMs EHR FEMR BM25
02
Fries Lab · Survival Analysis

Genomic Aberrations & Treatment History in Patient Survival

Examining how genomic aberrations and longitudinal treatment histories interact to influence patient survival outcomes. Combining structured EHR data with genomic features to build richer, more personalized prognostic models.

Survival Analysis Genomics EHR Prognosis
03
CV Course Project

Fairness in Dermatology AI: Skin Tone Disparities in Foundation Models

Evaluating inference-time preprocessing interventions — lesion segmentation and grayscaling — on frozen foundation models (DermLIP, OpenCLIP) to address skin-tone-based performance disparities in kNN retrieval classification across Fitzpatrick Skin Type groups using the DDI dataset.

Algorithmic Fairness Medical Imaging Foundation Models Dermatology DDI Dataset
04
Earlier Work · Medical Imaging

CT Scan Lung Segmentation

Medical imaging pipeline for automated lung segmentation from CT scans using pydicom and skimage. Early exposure to the full cycle of clinical image processing — from DICOM handling to morphological operations to anatomical boundary detection.

CT Scans Segmentation pydicom skimage
